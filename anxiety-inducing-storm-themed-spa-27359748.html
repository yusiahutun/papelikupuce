'Anxiety-inducing' storm-themed spa pool with rain and lightning splits opinion

A storm-themed spa room where thunderous clouds hover above the pool and rain pours onto guests has gone viral online.

Recently, a video clip of a storm-themed room, reportedly at the QC Termemilano Spa, went viral on Reddit.

Twitter user, @Rainmaker1973, also stated that the multi-sensory room was based in the Milan spa – though the website does not confirm its availabiliy.

The viral clip showed a swimming pool with stairs being drenched in rain from above as storm clouds rolled across the walls and lightening flashed.

A massive 111,000 people liked the post and thousands commented on the unusual spa experience.

Access to the QC Termemilano Spa in Milan costs between £32 and £43 with treatments and massages costing extra.

The QC Termemilano Spa in Milan, Italy, is surrounded by the Spanish Walls and blends beautiful architecture with high-tech experiences.

Not far from Porta Romana the spa offers a number of multi-sensory surroundings from the gorgeous Darsena Garden to a path that should be explored barefoot covered in: Carrara stones, fir cones, volcanic lava stones and bark.

There’s also a bio-sauna inside of a historic tram carriage, a pine-wood steam room and a shower room where you lie down on stone beds to be drenched from jets above your head.

Plus, there’s a water and salt room where the walls are formed of pink salt and crystal growths as well as a hay biosauna, a steam bath and the “thousand blue bubbles pools” outside with glass walls and whirlpools.

The Monte Tabor lets you relax in darkness only to be plunged into aromatic and visual treatments.

Access to the QC Termemilano Spa in Milan costs between £32 and £43 with treatments and massages costing extra.
