Widower sues hospital that released bi-polar wife a DAY before she murdered their three daughters 'despite warnings from experts'

  • Christine Adewunmi, 37, shot her three daughters in the head before shooting herself March 16, 2012
  • Wife and mother was evaluated at Mercy Hospital St Louis the day before and referred to outpatient program
  • Lawsuits filed by Leonard Adewunmi accuse hospital and on-call psychiatrist of negligence 

By Daily Mail Reporter

Mental problems: Christine Adewunmi, a 37-year-old stay-at-home mom from Clayton, Missouri, had been battling a bi-polar disorder, depression and had been experiencing suicidal thoughts

The husband of a Missouri woman suffering from a bi-polar disorder who shot dead the couple's three children and then herself last March is suing the hospital that had discharged her one day before the tragedy.

Christine Adewunmi, a 37-year-old stay-at-home mom from Clayton with a history of depression, shot Lauren, 8, Samantha, 6, and Kate, 3, in the head March 16, 2012, before turning the gun on herself, according to wrongful death complaints filed by Leonard Adewunmi this week.

The widower claims in his lawsuits against Mercy Hospital St. Louis, West County Physicians and Dr. Saad Khan that the day before the deadly incident, his wife arrived at the medical center's Behavioral Health Outpatient Department seeking treatment.

The complaints cited by the Courthouse News Service state that during the intake process, a hospital staffer learned that Mrs Adewunmi had sought psychiatric help only after being pressured by family members, who became concerned due to her worsening depression and bi-polar disorder.

The patient's initial evaluation also has revealed that the 37-year-old mother of three had been suicidal and had not taken her anti-depressants for two days. 

Leonard Adewunmi's complaints describe how the hospital worker, indented as Joann Bova, determined that his wife was willing to harm herself, that she resisted treatment and that both her father and brother had committed suicide.  

Family torn apart: Christine Adewunmi (center), seen here with her husband Leonard (back), shot and killed her daughters Lauren (left), Kate (center front) and Amanda (right) before killing herself

Innocent: Samantha Adewunmi, 6, and her sister Lauren, 8, were found dead at a rural Missouri campground. Police say  their mother Christine killed them and little sister Kate before turning the gun on herself

Bova then paged Dr Saad Khan, the on-call psychiatrist, who admitted his wife into the hospital's intensive outpatient program starting the following morning.

Christine Adewunmi was allowed to leave the hospital just over an hour after the intake process.

According to the lawsuits, no one informed Mr Adewunmi, who was present at the hospital at the time, about the plan to have his wife enrolled in the outpatient program the following morning.

When Adewunmi asked his wife about the results of her evaluation, she lied to him, saying that she does not need any additional treatment, and the couple returned home with their children. 

According to the widower, had the hospital staff bothered to speak to him directly that day, he would have told them that his wife's therapist had referred her for inpatient care; that Christine had previously taken their daughters on long trips unsupervised; that she had talked about her desire to die, and that she had been researching suicide methods online.

Under fire: Leonard Adewunmi is suing Mercy Hospital St. Louis for releasing his wife just one day before the tragedy that wiped out his family

Christine was scheduled to begin her outpatient treatment at 8am the next day, but she never made it to the hospital. Instead, the mentally unstable woman shot her three young daughters and then herself while her husband was at work.

In his complaints accusing the hospital and the on-call psychiatrist of negligence, Mr Adewunmi claims that had his wife been hospitalized on March 15, it is safe to say that she would not have been able to kill their children and herself the next day.   

Pictures of Christine, her husband Leonard Adewunmi, and their three angelic girls show the loving family that neighbors were familiar with.

The stay-at-home mom and former physical education teacher was known for taking the girls to the park and playing with them often in the family’s yard.

Inside, however, she was battling demons that even her own family were unable grasp.

Angelic: Samantha (left) and Lauren (right) were enrolled in the local elementary school and Kate (center) attended a preschool program

‘Depression is difficult to understand and a serious illness that is hard to comprehend,’ they said in a statement following the discovery of the four bodies.

‘No one knew the depths of her problem or could ever foresee this tragedy occurring. We cannot understand why this happened and are dealing with this tragic loss one minute at a time.’

Leonard Adewunmi knew that something was wrong on the night of March 16, when he came home from work and his wife and children were missing, prompting him to call 911. 

While talking to police, he told them that he feared she may be suicidal as a result of her bipolar diagnosis and depression.

He had taken Christine to Mercy Hospital for an evaluation the day before she disappeared with the girls - and she had decided that she would check herself in for treatment at 8am Friday.

When police searched the family's home computer during the missing persons investigation, they found a number of searches on how to kill yourself and information about depression.

Tragic: The entrance to the Blue Spring Ranch and Resort in Bourbon, Missouri, where the four bodies were found on Saturday afternoon

Crime scene: The victims were discovered at Blue Springs Ranch, a leisure resort on the Meramec River about 80 miles southwest of St. Louis

Leonard said that she had previously left with their daughters twice before but had returned, although she lied about her whereabouts each time.

He tried calling his wife’s cell phone more than 25 times, according to local newspaper St Louis Today.

Around 1pm the next day, a guest at Blue Springs Ranch discovered the bodies along a gravel road. A handgun was also found nearby.
