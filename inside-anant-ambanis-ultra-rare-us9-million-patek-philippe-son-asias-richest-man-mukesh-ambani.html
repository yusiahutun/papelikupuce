Inside Anant Ambanis ultra-rare US$9 million Patek Philippe: the son of Asias richest man Muke

The internet has been flooded with pictures and videos from the two-day-long event; however, there was one particular video that caught our eye as we managed to spot a very, very special Patek Philippe.

Any watch connoisseur worth their salt would be aware of the Patek Philippe Grandmaster Chime Ref. 6300G. It’s the most complicated Patek ever made, the Swiss watchmaker claims on its website. And with just a handful of them ever made, it’s almost impossible to spot one.

But a grand occasion calls for a special watch, and the person rocking the horological unicorn was none other than Anant Ambani, the youngest son of Mukesh Ambani – Asia’s richest man – and Nita Ambani.

Patek Philippe released the Grandmaster Chime Ref. 5175 as part of its 175th anniversary celebration in 2014. Priced at US$2.5 million, the incredible timepiece was touted as the most complicated watch the brand had ever made. What’s more, the watch was limited to just six pieces, all in ornately engraved yellow-gold cases.

However, two years later, Patek Philippe released the second iteration of the Grandmaster Chime, Ref. 6300G, which replaced the engraved yellow-gold case with a white-gold version along with some more changes.

More importantly, it was mechanically identical, making it just as special as the original. Patek made no more than a dozen of the Ref. 6300G with black and white dials before replacing it with a version that features a pair of blue dials along with a matching blue strap. Anant was spotted wearing the blue version of the timepiece.

Patek Philippe originally launched the Grandmaster Chime Ref. 6300G with a price tag of US$2.2 million. However, the prices of these watches have skyrocketed in the secondary market, with some examples available for close to US$9 million, per Chrono24.

But it’s arguably justifiable considering the fact that another iteration of the same timepiece (Ref. 6300A) currently holds the record for the most expensive watch ever sold. The one-of-a-kind watch set a world record in 2019 by selling for US$31 million, per Robb Report. The unique wristwatch has two dials, a reversible case and 20 complications. Patek Philippe spent eight years developing the original watch that came out in 2014.

The timepiece has four spring barrels driving 20 complications, including a grande and petite sonnerie, a minute repeater, an instantaneous perpetual calendar with a four-digit year display, second-time zone, day/night indicator, day/date (on both dials), month, leap-year cycle, four-digit year display and 24-hour and minute subdial.

Two of the complications were actually the first of their kind. The Grandmaster Chime holds six patents, including one for the mechanism that allows you to easily swivel and lock the double-faced case.
