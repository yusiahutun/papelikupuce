Fried Green Tomatoes

A great movie and an even better dish, crispy green tomatoes are part of every Southerner's palate. Tart and battered, these green guys are the ultimate fried food.

Ballantine Books Fannie Flagg's Original Whistle Stop Cafe Cookbook

2

Ballantine Books Fannie Flagg's Original Whistle Stop Cafe Cookbook

Who better to learn from than Fannie Flagg herself? As the author of the best-selling book and inspiration for the film Fried Green Tomatoes at the Whistle Stop Cafe, her companion cookbook promises you too can re-create the greens seen on screen.

3

Chicken and Dumplings

Manny Rodriguez

It doesn't matter how bad of a day you're having; when you come home to a dinner of chicken and dumplings everything is instantly easier. This comfy, cozy dish feels just as good as a hug from your grandma.

4

Brand: Ten Speed Press The Southern Slow Cooker

4

Brand: Ten Speed Press The Southern Slow Cooker

Because we might not always have grandma on hand, cooking chicken and dumplings in a slow cooker is one way to have this home-cooked meal ready when you come home. The recipe featured here is easy-to-make, time-friendly, and rivals any restaurant.

5

Sausage Biscuits and Gravy

PickStock

It might not be the prettiest, but there's no better breakfast. Biscuits and gravy is a dish you order without hesitation once you discover it's on the menu, and now there's no knead to go out to eat again after learning how to make it yourself.

6

Voyageur Press Biscuit Head: New Southern Biscuits, Breakfasts, and Brunch

6

Voyageur Press Biscuit Head: New Southern Biscuits, Breakfasts, and Brunch

Now 22% Off

As owners of the Biscuit Head restaurants that originated in Asheville, North Carolina, you can trust this husband-and-wife duo's recipe is top-tier. The book goes beyond biscuits, too. It includes directions for other Southern staples and has helpful tips (like using an ice cream scoop to measure your biscuits!).

7

Fried Chicken

Michael Tagoe

Place it on a sandwich for lunch or serve it as a side with waffles during breakfast—it doesn't get more Southern or versatile than the classic fried chicken. The dish fits into all three mealtimes for a delicious reason.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

8

Atlantic Monthly Press Shuck Beans, Stack Cakes, and Honest Fried Chicken

8

Atlantic Monthly Press Shuck Beans, Stack Cakes, and Honest Fried Chicken

This Kentucky-born cook definitely has the credibility for writing a fried chicken recipe. You're not only getting a taste of the South from her food, either. Author Ronni Lundy includes stories from her time in the region with her directions.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

9

Peach Cobbler

DebbiSmirnoff

When it comes to dessert, fruit should be center stage. At least that's true for Southern sweets. Fitting somewhere between a pie and a tart, peach cobbler is a quintessential summer treat.

10

Gibbs Smith Just Peachy

10

Gibbs Smith Just Peachy

There's nothing fuzzy about following this recipe—except for the fruit you'll use. The writer is a granddaughter of Mississippi sharecroppers and shares history and knowledge about peaches alongside her delicious directions.

11

Cooked Greens

Little Hand Images

Sometimes they're paired with cornbread and black-eyed peas as a dish meant to bring good luck in the New Year. Or, find them at a dinner featuring fried chicken and sweet potatoes. Either way, you can't go wrong serving greens at a Southerner's supper.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

12

Knopf In Pursuit of Flavor

12

Knopf In Pursuit of Flavor

Now 37% Off

You won't find someone who knows fresh, Southern flavor better than the award-winning author Edna Lewis. She knows every trick in the book, so she wrote her own. Edna shares recipes from her childhood in a Virginia farming community, including her flavorful cooked greens that feature just four ingredients.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

13

Banana Pudding

StephanieFrey

If you go to a BBQ and they aren't serving banana pudding, is it even a BBQ? Certainly not one in the South, where this dessert is necessary for cooling down after facing some hot heat from spicy rubs and high humidity.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

14

Atria Books Callie's Biscuits and Southern Traditions

14

Atria Books Callie's Biscuits and Southern Traditions

Carrie Morey is more than just biscuits, and she proves it in more ways than one. Before Callie's Hot Little Biscuits was a nationally recognized brand, her family passed down recipes amongst themselves. She shares some here, like one for Mama’s Sour Cream Banana Pudding.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

15

Fried Catfish

Jeff R Clow

It'd be pretty fishy to dine at a meat-and-three without at least one person in your group getting this fried dish. Flaky, crunchy, and maybe even a little spicy, there's something even more special about a homemade batch of Southern-fried catfish.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

16

Rodney Scott's World of BBQ

16

Rodney Scott's World of BBQ

World-renowned pitmaster Rodney Scott walks readers through his grandmother’s fried catfish recipe in this cookbook. Not only are you learning from a pro, but the sweet sentiment behind the dish makes it taste all the better.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

17

Okra

Photo by Cathy Scola

One of the most divisive dishes to date, people stand on a firm side of the aisle when asked their opinion on the veggie. But, with the right spices and seasonings, there's surely a way to find harmony even among the nonbelievers.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

18

The University of North Carolina Press Okra: a Savor the South cookbook

18

The University of North Carolina Press Okra: a Savor the South cookbook

If there's hope yet to convert your friends and family from hating okra to celebrating the seed, you'll find it here. While featuring a great Southern-fried recipe, there are plenty of other options for convincing, from grilled and steamed options to a gumbo recipe.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

19

Sweet Potato Pie

msheldrake

Swap potatoes for pumpkins, and you'll get a classic Southern pie. Forget saving this sweet only for Thanksgiving; the dessert is too good not to serve year-round.

20

Ten Speed Press Mrs. Rowe's Little Book of Southern Pies

This recipe is as easy as...well, you know. The no-fuss, no-frills directions promise the perfect pie, so you'll have time to make the other classic flavors the cookbook includes.
