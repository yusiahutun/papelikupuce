PGA tour

During his PGA Tour rookie season in 2011, Kirk finished joint second to Philip Mickelson at the Shell Houston Open. The first tour event he won was the Viking Classic which was held towards the end of the year. This was an alternate event that had been held the same weekend as The Open Championship. He had won by finishing a stroke ahead of Tom Pernice Jr. and George McNeil, so he qualified for the PGA Championship.

In his debut season on the PGA Tour, Kirk finished in a tie for 34th place. However, he registered four top-10s and finished 42nd in the FedEx Cup standings. This meant he had retained his tour card for the 2012 season. He played in 27 events in the 2012 season and was largely successful.

The 2013 season was no different from the 2012 one for Kirk, who continued with his impressive form. He missed only four cuts and had three top-10 finishes. In addition, he was the runner-up of the AT&T Pebble Beach National Pro-Am, finishing two shots behind Brandt Snedeker.
