Michelle Obama Stars in New Funny or Die Video with Nick Offerman and Megan Mullally

In honor of the 60th anniversary of the President's Council on Fitness, Sports & Nutrition, Michelle Obama is celebrating with a touch of humor. The First Lady stars in a new video by Funny or Die with actor and comedian Nick Offerman (better known to Parks and Rec. fans as Ron Swanson).

The bit, called "The History of Exercise" (perhaps a reference to Obama's "Evolution of Mom Dancing" skits with Jimmy Fallon?) goes over the evolution of workouts through the years, starting as far back as the 1950's. Offerman walks through exercise trends by the decade, but when he get's too tired, a vision of FLOTUS comes to his rescue:

"Nick, you can do it! Get back out there exercise!" she encourages him, but the ego boost doesn't stop there.

"And your wife is amazing. I love her!" she squealed, referencing Offerman's spouse Megan Mullally, who also makes a cameo in the video.

Watch the full clip below.

This isn't the FLOTUS' first time working with Funny or Die; it actually marks her third year in a row of appearing on the comedy sketch site. She was in a skit about healthy snacks (starring Chloe Grace Moretz) in 2014, and another on grocery shopping (with Billy Eichner and Sesame Street's Big Bird) in 2015.
