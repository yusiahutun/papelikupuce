NYPD detective says she was ordered to hand over her gun after she reported her boss assaulted her

A female detective with the New York Police Department’s Hate Crime Task Force said she was punished after she filed a complaint against her boss for assault.

Detective Nela Gomez said she went to her boss Lt. Robert Delaney on August 31 about a subpoena case she was working on when he launched into an expletive-filled rant, kicked her out of his office and then allegedly slammed the door so hard it hit her and caused her to fall to the ground.

‘While I was in the office, (Delaney) starts yelling: “Where are going? You’re not going anywhere! Overtime is going to be done here,”’ she told the Daily News.

She said Delaney appeared to be in a bad mood.

Detective Nela Gomez (left) said she was physically assaulted by her boss Lt. Robert Delaney (right) last month 

Gomez said she went into her bosses office for a case she was working on and he started cursing at her and kicked her out of his office 

Gomez said the door hit her and caused her to fall. The 13-year veteran said she suffered bruises on her back and leg

Gomez said she showed pictures of her bruises to Internal Affairs but was punished instead of Delaney 

‘He’s yelling in my face,’ she said. ‘He’s six-foot-two. I’m five-foot-seven. It’s jibberish. He’s yelling at me without any substance. He’s saying: “I’m f***ing done with you! This is f***ing over!”’

Gomez said that she tried asking her boss what she had done wrong and he ordered out of his office. She claimed as she made her way out of the room Delaney, 51, slammed the door causing the edge to clip her. 

'The door hits me, and now I fly to the floor and land on the corner my left side,' she told ABC News. 'Now I'm devastated and terrified.'

Gomez said she suffered bruises on her arm, leg and back from the door hitting her. She said immediately contacted Internal Affairs about the physical assault. 

She told the Daily News that Delaney tried to downplay the incident to Inspector Mark Molinari, the Hate Crimes commander.

She said the inspector wanted to investigate the incident as a verbal dispute, but she interjected and said she was assaulted by her supervisor.

 Gomez, a 13-year veteran, said she was interviewed by Captain Thomas Kamper from Manhattan South Inspections and was told that she couldn’t file a complaint or press charges against Delaney. She thinks she was told she couldn’t file a complaint because Delaney is a lieutenant.

The detective said if her boss was an average citizen ‘an arrest warrant would be made’.

‘Especially with injuries,’ she added to the Daily News. ‘Then it would be up to the courts. It seems like they are protecting the lieutenant. Why do the rules change because he’s a cop? We’re supposed to uphold the law and this is what they do and they wonder why people don’t trust cops.’ 

Gomez said despite having photographic evidence of her bruises, she was punished for speaking out and Delaney was not reprimanded at all.

Delaney (pictured) denied hitting Gomez with the door 

Gomez said she was forced to take a psychological evaluation, was stripped of her weapon and shield and placed on desk duty. 

'They made me feel like I did something wrong,' she told ABC. 'I was penalized. My gun was taken, not his. I had to undergo a psych evaluation. He didn't.'

'They re-victimized me,' she added.    

Gomez said she was restored to full duty and transferred to Manhattan Special Victims unit after passing the evaluation.

Lou Turco, president of the Lieutenants Benevolent Association, said Gomez allegedly started the incident when she became irate that Delaney told her to work on the subpoena case from the office.

‘She thought she needed to go to the scene,’ he told the Daily News, also denying that Gomez was hit by the door.

‘The door was never slammed to cause the force to hit her with the door,’ he told the outlet. ‘We’re adamant that no injury was caused by any door hitting her.’

‘The lieutenant’s account is backed up by two independent witnesses,’ Turco added.

Gomez described the environment at the Hate Crimes Unit as toxic and said women transfer out of there after only a few months on the job.

‘I was told “When you get your period, you better not be a b****,”’ Gomez said. ‘I always wondered why women would go there and leave after a few months and now I know. It’s clear they don’t want women in that unit.’

A police spokesman said the case is an active investigation.
