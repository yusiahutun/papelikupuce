Kate Middleton Wore Her Go-To Outfit-Elevating Winter Staple

It feels like, out of nowhere, the temperature just dropped. I was still getting away with short sleeves under my cardigans and jackets, but it quickly turned to chunky sweaters under my puffiest coats. I always feel like I lose my sense of chicness as it gets colder because I’m always concealed under bubble coats, but Kate Middleton is someone who always makes me rethink throwing on a sweatsuit and actually dressing up to brave the cold. I know being a princess means abiding by a strict dress code, but I find it refreshing that her looks are so easy to replicate for the everyday dresser looking to jazz up their seasonal attire. 

Middleton was recently spotted wearing a belted midi-length skirt, knee-high boots, and a wardrobe staple we have seen her in on a number of occasions: a black turtleneck and, more specifically, a Gabriela Hearst Peppe Ribbed Cashmere Turtleneck that retails for a steep $790. She’s constantly out and about mixing and mingling, braving the cold weather — and if she’s reaching for turtlenecks (even pricier ones), I’m also adding a few more to my rotation — and you should, too. I rounded up 10 classic black turtlenecks that can be paired with midi and maxi skirts, your favorite jeans, or under dresses from Spanx, J.Crew, and Banana Republic — starting at $24.

Amazon is my favorite place to shop for closet basics without breaking the bank. Its also easy to layer under flannels, coats, and jackets.

Skims Fits Everybody Funnel Neck Top

I have the Skims Fits Everybody Funnel Neck Top in multiple colors because it's super easy to style.
