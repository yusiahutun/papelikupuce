Early life

Cheryl Scott was born on 19 January 1985 in New Jersey, United States. She is the daughter of Marie Picciano Scott (mother) and has two older brothers, Kevin Scott and Brian Scott. She grew up in Atco, Camden County, New Jersey. She had an interest in science while growing up.

After graduating from elementary school, Cheryl attended Paul VI High School, a private Catholic high school in Haddon Township, in Camden County. She loved sports and supported the Philadelphia Eagles. She was also a soccer player for her high school’s track and field programme.

Cheryl was a fast runner while in high school. Her athleticism on the track and field attracted the attention of several university recruiters. After graduating high school, she enrolled at Brown University, a private Ivy League research university in Providence, Rhode Island.

The personality graduated from university in 2007 with a Bachelor’s degree in Geological Science from Brown University. After that, Cheryl Scott enrolled at Mississippi State University, where she received the Meteorology Certification in 2010.

Professional career

Before receiving her Meteorology Certification, Cheryl interned at WCAU-NBC 10 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, United States. In 2007 after graduating, she became a weekend weathercaster at WSEE-TV in Erie, Pennsylvania. She also provided forecasts for a 24-hour Caribbean Weather Channel.

After receiving her Meteorology Certification, Cheryl Scott went to Knoxville, Tennessee. She landed a job as a weekend meteorologist for the NBC affiliate network, WBIR-TV. In April 2011, Scott was on the air when several tornadoes destroyed Knoxville.

People praised Scott during this time for updating them on the storm developments. She became a crucial source of information and helped thousands stay safe. Additionally, Cheryl volunteered for the Red Cross after the disaster.

In September 2011, Cheryl left Knoxville and relocated to Chicago. She joined NBC 5 in Chicago as the morning and weekend meteorologist. She worked for NBC 5 for three years. In December 2014, Cheryl Scott joined ABC7 Eyewitness News in Chicago. She has worked for ABC7 Eyewitness News since then.
