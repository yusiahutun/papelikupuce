The CBum Workout Routine to be Sculpted Like a Bodybuilder

Cbum's workout routine is the secret behind this famous bodybuilder, also known as Chris Bumstead’s success in the Men's Classic Physique division. He is known for his impressive physique that refreshes our memories of the Golden Era of bodybuilding. His workout plan focuses on building a balanced and proportionate physique with an emphasis on muscle density, symmetry, and aesthetics.

CBum is the reigning Mr. Olympia Classic Physique winner which is a testament to how challenging his workout and diet regime is. So, in this article, we will discuss various aspects of his lifestyle because although constructing a physique deserving of such accolades may not be your goal, the discipline, hard work, and dedication that CBum displays in his life may offer you an inspiration to follow your own fitness journey. 

Who is Chris Bumstead?

Christopher Adam Bumstead, affectionately known as CBum by his fans, is a professional bodybuilder from Canada affiliated with the IFBB. Born on February 2, 1995, in Ottawa, Ontario, Bumstead grew up in a sporting environment. During his high school years, he engaged in various sports, such as fencing, football, baseball, basketball, and hockey. 

Chris Bumstead’s weightlifting journey started at the early age of 14 when he started working out extensively and went from 170 to 225 pounds. As Bumstead's aspirations in bodybuilding began to escalate, his sister's boyfriend, Iain Valliere, recognized his potential and provided guidance for his various competitions. There he unexpectedly found himself securing high rankings, even placing second at the prestigious Olympia event. Bumstead's journey in bodybuilding commenced with a regional-level competition in Ontario, which he entered alongside his sister, Melissa Valliere. To their delight, both emerged victorious, with Bumstead achieving the junior category win. This experience deepened his passion for bodybuilding, leading him to collaborate directly with Iain and dedicate himself fully to the sport.

In 2014, at the age of 19, Bumstead debuted in competitive bodybuilding. At 21, he earned his IFBB professional status after clinching victory at the 2016 IFBB North American Bodybuilding Championship. His inaugural appearance at the Mr. Olympia event in 2017 left a remarkable impression, securing him second place in the Classic Physique category. In 2018, he retained his Mr. Olympia title despite the setback he faced when he was hospitalized four weeks prior to the competition due to severe water retention stemming from kidney issues. His physical condition was less optimal but he continued with his training.

Bumstead's global recognition soared as he clinched the title of Mr. Olympia consecutively in 2019, 2020, 2021, and 2022. These victories established him as the reigning champion in the Men's Classic Physique division, solidifying his place in bodybuilding history.
