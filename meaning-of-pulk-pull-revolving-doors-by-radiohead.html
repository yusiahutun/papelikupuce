Meaning of Pulk/pull Revolving Doors by Radiohead

In the surreal and enigmatic world of Radiohead's "Pulk/pull Revolving Doors," the theme of duality and the ever-present existence of choices takes center stage. This hauntingly beautiful track explores the intricate nature of decisions, the paths we choose, and the consequences that follow. As we dissect the standout lyrics of this song, we uncover unexpected themes that add a layer of depth to the overall message.The opening lines immediately draw our attention to the dichotomy between stability and fluidity. "There are barn doors" represents a sense of permanence and solidity, while "there are revolving doors" signifies constant motion and change. This play on opposites highlights the unpredictable nature of life and the choices we face. It reminds us that even the most seemingly straightforward decisions can lead to unexpected outcomes.Moving further into the song, we encounter the idea of doors and their unique characteristics. The line "Doors in the rudders of big ships and there are revolving doors" presents an intriguing image of doors within doors. This suggests the possibility of hidden choices within our decisions, revealing paths we never thought existed. It prompts us to reflect on how our choices can shape not only our own lives but also have far-reaching consequences for others.The following verse delves into the concept of automated doors, which can open without our intervention. "There are doors that open by themselves" presents a theme of fate or destiny, signaling that some choices may be beyond our control. This raises questions about free will and the extent to which we are truly in charge of our own lives. It invites contemplation on the interplay between choice and external forces, leaving room for interpretations of predestination or cosmic influence.The mention of sliding and secret doors adds another layer of symbolism to the song. Sliding doors, often associated with uncertainty and ambiguity, remind us that there are paths not readily apparent or easily accessible. These hidden corridors suggest the presence of secrecy and mystery in our decision-making processes. They encourage us to look beyond the surface and consider the hidden motives and intentions that drive our choices.As we approach the final verse, the song delves into doors that lock, doors that don't, and doors that let you in and out but never open. These lines evoke a sense of entrapment, capturing the frustration and helplessness that can arise from certain decisions. They speak to the realization that some choices may seem promising on the surface, but ultimately lead to dead ends or unfulfilled expectations. This theme of doors that don't open calls attention to the importance of discernment and self-awareness in decision-making.The climax of the song arrives with the mention of trapdoors, representing choices that have irreversible consequences. "And there are trapdoors that you can't come back from" signifies the gravity of certain decisions and the irreversible paths they set us on. This chilling realization underscores the importance of careful consideration and foresight in navigating life's possibilities. It serves as a reminder that not all choices can be undone, and we must approach them with caution and mindfulness.In summary, Radiohead's "Pulk/pull Revolving Doors" invites us into a thought-provoking exploration of choices, duality, and the consequences that stem from our decisions. Through an assortment of vivid and imaginative lyrics, the song sheds light on unexpected themes such as hidden choices, fate, secrecy, entrapment, and irreversible consequences. With every door opened or closed, we are faced with the intricate maze of life's choices, each path leading us to new and unforeseen destinations. As we navigate our personal revolving doors, may we approach them with curiosity, awareness, and a willingness to embrace the unpredictable journey.
