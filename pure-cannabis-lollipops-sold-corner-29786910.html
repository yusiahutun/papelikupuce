'Pure Cannabis lollipops' sold at corner shop for 40p each as 'smokers choice'

Police discovered cannabis-laced lollipops being sold for just 40p each at a corner shop.

Cops raided a convenience store in Cambridgeshire last April where they found the sweets.

The shop on Market Street in Whittlesey had lollipops containing hemp oil and bubblegum. They were advertised with cannabis leaves on the box.

The packaging featured images of bongs, mushrooms and a picture of a spliff. It contained the words "smokers choice".

The lollipops were confiscated and destroyed. Before that, they were found to contain THC (Tetrahydrocannabinol), a derivative of cannabis.

Shopkeeper Gulal Shamalzi, 26, was later found guilty of possessing cannabis with intent to supply. He admitted the offence in Peterborough Magistrates' Court.

Shamalzi was handed a 12-month community order with mental health treatment and 12 days of rehabilitation. He was ordered to do 100 hours of unpaid community service.
