List of edible snakes and where they are consumed in the world

Many people fear snakes because there are some which are venomous. However, some see snakes as a delicious and nutritious meal. Edible snakes offer a myriad of health benefits. So, how much do you know about these snakes?

You may have probably heard stories about the Chinese eating snakes. If yes, have you stopped to ask whether it is true or false, or which type of snakes they eat? It is true that some Chinese and people from other races worldwide consume some snakes. Here's everything you should know about edible snakes.

List of edible snakes

Can you eat any kind of snake? Not all snakes are edible. Therefore, when it comes to choosing an edible snake, it really depends on credible information lest you cause harm to yourself. According to Afro Gist media, these are the edible snakes.

1. Copperhead

Copperhead snakes are some of the more commonly seen snakes. They have an elegant copper and black colour. They are the most likely to bite, although their venom is relatively mild, and their bites are rarely fatal for humans.

Copperhead snakes have a yellow-tipped tail when young. They use it to lure prey and can live for up to 18 years. Being a part of the pit viper group, they also have a blocky head and a hefty body. They are social and active during summer but only during day time.

2. Cottonmouth

Cottonmouths, also called water moccasins, are venomous snakes found in the southeastern United States. They are called cottonmouths because of the white colouration on the inside of their mouths. They display this white colouration when threatened.

The snakes have a black-brown shade and possess black bands which are dense on the top part of the snake’s body, narrowing down its body sides. They are semiaquatic, so they are comfortable both swimming in water and basking on land. They are pit vipers as are copperheads and rattlesnakes.

3. Coral snakes

Coral snakes are a large group of elapid snakes. They are subdivided into two distinct groups; Old World coral snakes and New World coral snakes. The snake’s colourings are usually red on yellow on black.

However, the red and yellow colours must touch each other to enhance the snake's venom. These snakes are popularly in Asia and North America. Unlike other snake species, coral snakes do not grow big and may range from 3 to 5 feet long.

Coral snakes mostly feed on frogs and blind snakes. Even though they are claimed to be venomous, their bite has a lesser likelihood of causing death, but it is painful.

4. Rattlesnakes

Rattlesnakes are predators that live in an array of habitats. They hunt small animals such as birds and rodents for food. Their most recognisable feature is the rattle toward their tail end. It has an unmistakable sound and will rattle if bothered.

There are sixteen different rattlesnakes species , and they grow between 0.9 cm to 1.2 cm long. They are active during the day and night in spring and hibernate during winter. They are found in the southern part of the United States.

Preparation steps

The meat of snakes can be extremely tough if not properly prepared. So, follow the steps below if you want to have an amazing meal:

  • Refrigerate the meat as soon as possible.
  • Cut off the snake's head, strip off the skin, and remove the guts of the dead snake.
  • Rinse the meat, and cut it into pieces with a sharp knife or poultry shears.
  • Dip the segments in a bit of egg white before dredging them in a pepper and sweet cornmeal mix.
  • Heat about 3/4 inch (2 cm) of canola, vegetable, or peanut oil in a heavy frying pan until quite hot.
  • Remove the snake pieces before they are quite done - they will continue to cook after removal from the pan - and set them on paper towels to drain and cool.
  • Serve your fried snake bits warm.
