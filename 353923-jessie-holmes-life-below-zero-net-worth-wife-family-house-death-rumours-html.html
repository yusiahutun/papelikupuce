Career

How was Jessie Holmes' past? The sledge dog racer left his home in Alabama to visit Alaska at 16 and lived there for a while. Later, he became a carpenter for three years in Montana. He then saved the money he made from carpentry to facilitate his eventual permanent move to Alaska.

Later, he moved to Alaska with his dog, Freedom, where he built boats, sledges, cabins, and fish wheels. He first settled in Eagle before moving to Nenana, where he lives along the Nenana River.

He spends most of his time with his brood of at least 40 dogs. He breeds the dogs, trains them and ensures they are in good health. He also spends time fishing and hunting. He fishes at least 3,000 Solomon fish in the summer to sustain himself at the brood during winter.

Jessie Holmes' dogs have participated in and won numerous sledge competitions. Holmes's popularity rose significantly in 2015 when he was named one of the cast members of Life Below Zero. The BBC show is about his lifestyle in Alaska's freezing wilderness, and it airs on National Geographic.

Why did Jessie Holmes leave Life Below Zero?

In October 2022, the renowned sledge dog racer was injured on his way to Golovin, Alaska, in the company of his friends. The group was part of a concerted effort to clean up after the effects of Typhoon Merbok that had ravaged the western coast of Alaska.

Jessie Holmes' accident reportedly saw him suffer a few broken bones, forcing him to be airlifted to Nome and then to Anchorage. His friends created a GoFundMe page to help raise the amount required for Jessie's surgery. The appeal had raised more than $40,000 in less than a week.
