Florence Pugh's Ball Gown Includes Hip Cutouts and a Low-Cut Back

Florence Pugh just delivered one of her most glamorous red-carpet looks to date.

At the U.K. premiere of Christopher Nolan’s Oppenheimer—in which Pugh stars alongside Cillian Murphy and Emily Blunt—the actor wowed in a showstopping dress fresh off the Valentino runway.

The fiery red ball gown, which hails from the Italian designer’s Fall/Winter 2023 haute couture collection, features a figure-hugging drop-waist silhouette, a plunging V-neckline, long cutouts near the waist, and a low-cut back tied off with a paneled draping detail. Pugh wore the look with strappy black heels and diamond-and-gold hoop earrings from Tiffany & Co. She upped the ante with her other jewelry, also pulled from Tiffany, including a gold rope ring and a yellow-diamond halo engagement ring.

Pugh often shows out in Valentino, with some of her most dramatic looks ever being the creations of the brand’s creative director, Pier Paolo Piccioli.

Most recently, the Don’t Worry Darling star sat front row at Valentino’s couture runway show during Paris Fashion Week. At the time, she opted to wear a wispy see-through maxi dress in a pale lavender hue. The gauzy dress featured delicate pleating details at the shoulders that extended into a ruffled collar.

And back in May, Pugh attended the Met Gala with Piccioli, dazzling in an elegant ivory gown with a plunging scooped neckline tied off with a dainty black tulle bow. The dress came with a towering headpiece topped with black feathers.
