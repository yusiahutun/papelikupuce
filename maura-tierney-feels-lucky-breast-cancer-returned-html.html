Maura Tierney feels very lucky her breast cancer hasn't returned

'I knock on wood': ER star Maura Tierney feels very lucky her breast cancer hasn't returned

By Linda Massarella

Published: 20:34 EDT, 2 July 2012 | Updated: 02:41 EDT, 3 July 2012

13

View
comments


As an actor on the hit show ER for 10 years, Maura Tierney played a very realistic nurse, then doctor. 

So realistic that when she went to her personal physician in 2009 for a small lump in her breast, the oncologist put the mammogram on the wall, assuming she would be able to read the devastating results for herself.

'It was the most surreal, crazy moment,' the 47-year-old recalled in an interview to be published in the upcoming issue of People Magazine.

In remission: Maura Tierney, pictured here in New York in January, is back on her feet after a bout with breast cancer and will join the cast of The Good Wife

The mammogram showed the actress, who played the character of very caring doctor Abby Lockhart, had cancer.

But, she said, 'of course I wouldn't know that by looking at it!' 

At 44, the diagnosis was of early stage breast cancer with a high survival rate.

She opted for a mastectomy, which left her skin intact for reconstructive surgery.


Realistic: As Nurse Abby Lockhart, Maura was so convincing that her own oncologist thought she was capable of reading her own mammogram

The diagnosis came one year after Maura told her ER fans she was quitting the show after 10 seasons.

'I wasn't angry and never said, "Why me?" But I was scared,' she said, especially when doctors advised her to undergo three months
of chemotherapy once they took out the tumour.

'My prognosis is good, but I knock on wood.'

Still good friends: Maura and former ER co-star Laura Innes were seen having a heart-to-heart in New York in January

Schedule conflicts with her cancer treatments led her to leave the cast of Parenthood before she even began. Once she recovered, she returned to television to play a recurring character on Rescue Me.

Today, Tierney is still in remission and looking forward to a meaty role on the hit CBS show, The Good Wife.

She'll play a 'self made millionaire who has become the doyenne of Chicago Democratic politics' - and be paired once again
with former ER actress and the star of The Good Wife, Julianna Margulies.
