Wait, What?!Demi Moore Confirms That She Really Doesnt Have Front Teeth

It seems Demi Moore wasn’t kidding, she really has no front teeth!

In a candid new interview, the A-list actress opened up about her decision to show a toothless photo of herself on live television. “I think it was literally just me being silly you know? It was a truthful thing,” Demi told Us Weekly. “We are all human and everything is all relative. Sometimes it’s nice to share so that people see all of you.”

The Striptease star previously made an appearance on The Tonight Show with Jimmy Fallon on Monday, June 12 — and shared something with fans that nobody ever knew about the very private star! Jimmy held up a selfie of Demi without her front teeth for all the audience to see…

Before the photo was shown, Jimmy, speechless, immediately asked her, “You sent us a photo and it’s the most insane thing I’ve ever seen. How did this happen?”

The 54-year-old actress then explained the situation, sharing that in the photo she’s only missing one tooth, but in real life, she’s actually missing both front teeth. “I sheared off my front teeth. I’d love to say it was skateboarding or something really kind of cool, but I think I think it’s something that’s important to share because I think it’s literally, probably after heart disease, one of the biggest killers in America, which is stress,” she confessed. “Stress sheared off my front teeth. But, in an effort to get ready for you, I wanted to make sure my teeth were in.”

Demi added that she “literally knocked her tooth out” recently. “It was almost like it fell out and my warranty was up. I swear!” she joked. “Thank God for modern dentistry. All of you who go out and practice modern dentistry—thank God!” Jimmy proceeded to show a funny clip of the star on Snapchat singing in a high pitch voice with a filter at the dentist.

Fortunately for Demi, her three daughters, Rumer, 28, Scout, 25, and Tallulah, 23, get a kick out of seeing their famous mom with her big gap! “They love seeing me without my teeth,” the Now and Then actress admitted. “Because they think it makes me look more vulnerable and more human.”

“I’m sitting there lisping and they’re making fun of me like I’m an eight year old waiting for my tooth to grow back in!” she added.
