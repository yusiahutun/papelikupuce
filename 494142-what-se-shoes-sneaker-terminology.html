What does SE mean in shoes? All about the sneaker terminology

When shopping for sneakers, you may have noticed that some sneakers have various terminologies in their description. For example, you will see terms like GS to mean Grade School, PE to imply Player Exclusive, H/O to represent Highest Offer, and VNDS to mean Very Near Deadstock. These terminologies often confuse buyers who see them for the first time. For example, one abbreviation you may have come across is SE. But what does SE mean in shoes?

In the sneaker business, SE terminology stands for Special Edition. It also refers to footwear that a sneaker brand has released in limited quantities, often with unique features that make the shoe stand out from the regular models.

SE sneakers are usually highly sought-after. Thus, they come with extra add-ons that add to the overall value of the shoes. Examples of add-ons that come with SE sneakers include special packaging, hangtags, and exclusive materials. Depending on the manufacturer, SE sneakers can come in exclusive colourways and other unique designs.

What does SE mean in shoes size?

SE is the general term that means Special Edition in sneakers. The most common SE size in sneakers is the medium width, denoted as M or B. However, some SE sneakers may come in other widths, such as narrow (N) or wide (D).

The width measurement is crucial because it affects the fit and comfort of the shoe. Therefore, when selecting a size, check the width of the shoe first, then determine the length.

However, you may notice that when buying SE sneakers online, the store will brand them as True To Size. The term means that the size of the sneaker corresponds to the size of your foot.

So, for example, if you typically wear a shoe size 8, a sneaker marked as True To Size will fit your shoe properly without sizing it up or down to get a comfortable fit.

The Air Jordan 1 has easy sizing because it fits True To Size. Therefore, if you wear shoe size 42 EU, an equivalent of US Men's 8.5, US Women's 10, and UK's 7.5, the shoe will fit you perfectly. But you will also notice that some SE sneakers do not have the True To Size label.

Some have the label Size Down 1/2 A Size. In this case, the label means ½ size Small, indicating the shoe size runs small. Therefore, if you wear a regular size 8, your ideal shoes will be 8.5 for shoes that run ½ size small. And if you see ½ size Large, the shoe runs large. Thus, your regular size 8 shoes will be 7.5.

SE sneakers are highly desirable and sought after because of their unique attributes. Generally, shoe brands release the sneakers at high-profile events or special occasions, making them hard to find.

What is the difference between SE and LE?

While SE means Special Edition, LE means Limited Edition in the sneaker business. Generally, SE is sneakers limited in styles or colourways. Also, brands produce SE sneakers for a smaller group or in smaller amounts, unlike in the regular edition of the same sneakers. In addition, they often feature unique accents or materials that make them stand out.

What does GS and SP stand for in sneaker terminology?

GS stands for Grade School. It is a term attached to sneakers appropriate for children. PS stands for Preschool. PS sizes are typically smaller than GS sizes as they are produced for younger children.
