The Psychology of Money and Happiness

Apr 13, 2024 “Money doesn’t buy happiness or does it?” In 2010, psychologist Daniel Kahneman and economist Angus Deaton attempted to investigate if money impacted people’s psychological well-being, which comprises positive and negative effects and life satisfaction. The study found that money did impact how people evaluate their lives when they think about it; that people with more money feel better about their lives. However, emotional well-being rose with income, as expected too, but only to an annual salary of $75,000 ($90,000 in today’s money).
