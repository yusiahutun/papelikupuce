James Corden told pal Mathew Horne refused interview with him in resurfaced clip

James Corden may be a big comedian and host an American chat show but to those in the UK he will always be best known for playing the iconic character of Smithy in Gavin and Stacey.

He starred alongside Mathew Horne who was his on screen best friend but when the show ended, rumours began circulating that James had fallen out with his co-star and pal Matthew after his move to the States.

A resurfaced clip has shown that Mathew refused to do an interview alongside James when he appeared on Piers Morgan's Life Stories.

In 2019 James was told that Mathew had been asked to appear alongside him but said no.

In the clip Piers asked James: "Do you talk much?"

James replied: "Err, we talk occasionally, yeah."

Morgan then said: "We asked him to take part in this programme and he chose not to."

James looked shocked and said: "I didn't know that, but I can understand."

However even though it seemed that Mathew had taken issue with James, he later confirmed that was not the case.

Speaking to the Times, he said: "That's where the rumour that we'd fallen out started and it snowballed from there.

"I didn’t go on that show because I was filming out of London, and they could only film my interview on one day, and I couldn’t do it.

"The other reason is that I dislike Piers Morgan and everything he stands for."

He went on to confirm that he never fell out with James and said that they "talk quite a lot" given the time difference between America and the UK.

This comes after James was reportedly left "fuming" after being thrown from his rented bike in London.

The Late Late Show host took a tumble when he went out for a ride with his wife Julia, but luckily he did not seem to be hurt.

The couple were cycling down Regent Street when a young cyclist suddenly cut across James' path.

He was forced to swerve and apparently went "flying", reported The Sun.

A video obtained by the paper showed the bike on its side in the street and James, who did not appear to be injured, walking over and picking it up.

The star, 43, was then said to have confronted the other rider, who apologised for what happened.

"Corden was fuming," a source was quoted as saying.

"This young cyclist just darted across the road and sent him flying. It must have really taken him by surprise. He had no cuts or bruises but it must have hurt.

"He ticked off the cyclist who said 'sorry'."
