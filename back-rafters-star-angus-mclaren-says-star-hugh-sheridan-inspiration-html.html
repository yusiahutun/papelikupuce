Back To The Rafters star Angus McLaren says co-star Hugh Sheridan 'is an inspiration

It's been an emotional year for one of Australia's most popular actors, Hugh Sheridan.

In January, The Packed to the Rafters star, 36, who identifies as non-binary and goes by the pronouns they/them, got engaged to their fiancé Kurt Roberts.

At the time, they told The Daily Telegraph, they opted to propose to their partner after three months of dating as they'd found out their late father Denis Sheridan had been diagnosed with terminal cancer. 

Big year: Hugh Sheridan has had an emotional year. Within 12 months the actor has experienced the loss of their father Denis (pictured) and got engaged to the love of their life Kurt Roberts

In May - two months after getting engaged - the actor and singer's father tragically passed away at age 76.

Despite experiencing so many highs and lows within one year, Hugh's Back To The Rafters co-star Angus McLaren said Hugh's spirit did not falter.  

Speaking to Daily Mail Australia this week, Angus said: 'He [they] really is a prime example of someone who is in control of their reactions. He [they] makes great things out of sometimes really hard situations.'

'He [They] is in an inspiration, it is a responsibility to make sure you bring a good vibe to those around you and he does that.' 

Proud friend: Speaking to Daily Mail Australia this week, Angus said: 'He [They] really is a prime example of someone who is in control of their reactions. He [They]  makes great things out of sometimes really hard situations'

Following the death of their father, Hugh returned to the set of the year's most anticipated reboots Back To The Rafters.

'We just had such a ball on set,' added Angus, who plays Nathan, the brother of Hugh's character Ben Rafter.

'I guess Ben is similar to Hugh like that, very positive.'

Positive vibes: 'We just had such a ball on set,' added Angus, who plays Nathan, the brother of Hugh's character Ben Rafter

And it appears Hugh's attitude aided the star in finding success in their career. 

As Back To The Rafters premieres on Amazon Prime, Hugh is currently shooting a new film in Queensland. 

In their personal life, things are equally as good, too.

Hugh previously told The Daily Telegraph that they'll officially tie the knot with Kurt in Sydney on New Year's Eve. 

In March, Hugh proposed to Kurt during the first night of a dance performance at the Adelaide Fringe Festival.

Highs and lows: Hugh Sheridan shared the heartbreaking reason behind their decision to propose to boyfriend Kurt Roberts in March

How sweet: Hugh proposed to Kurt, 29, during the first night of a dance performance at the Adelaide Fringe Festival

'I went out and bought a ring and flew his family to be seated in the audience on the night. He had no idea. It was a really amazing moment,' Hugh gushed at the time.

After proposing, the loved-up pair shared a public embrace and a cheeky kiss before a besotted Hugh ran back on stage and said: 'I just got engaged!'

A friend of the couple's shared footage from the magical moment on Instagram, writing: 'It's not every day one of your besties proposes to the love of his life so excited for you both xx.' 

The couple plan to wed at the end of this year.
