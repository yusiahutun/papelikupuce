Family Bank Limited, Kenya branches, BIC / SWIFT Code, branch codes

Kenyans living abroad often find it difficult to transact, save, invest or get information about investment opportunities that exist back home. Diaspora banking allows Kenyans in the diaspora to conveniently enjoy the same banking opportunities that they would have accessed if they were still at home. Family Bank Limited is one of the banks serving the Kenyan diaspora population.

Family Bank is a fully-fledged commercial bank since May 2007. The bank is regulated by the Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) and is a member of the Kenya Deposit Insurance Corporation. It has a network of 93 branches countrywide. Family Bank has gone down in history as the first Bank in Kenya to introduce paperless banking through smart card technology.

About Family Bank Limited

The bank began as a society in the year 1984. At that time, it was known as the Family Finance Building Society. Its primary objective was to cater for the needs of the population that were not being met by the banks at the time. At that time, banking services were not affordable to ordinary people, and the bank stepped in to fill this gap.

In 2007, it became a fully-fledged bank. That would have seemed like the most significant achievement at the time, but it was only the beginning of its success story. In 2013, the bank had its first biggest milestone. This moment set the pace as the bank has been honoured severally, including being feted as the Fastest Growing Bank.

This positioning was based on Financials in 2015. The bank had a portfolio worth 2.9 Billion from 2.84 Billion from the previous year. It has also been named the Best Bank in Micro-Finance, among other honours. The introduction of products and services to cater for the ordinary mwananchi has been a substantial contributing factor to its growth.

Family Bank swift code

A SWIFT code is a standard format for Business Identifier Codes (BIC). Banks and financial institutions use them to identify themselves globally. When transferring money between banks, you will need these codes, particularly for international money transfers or SEPA payments. Banks also use these codes to exchange messages with each other.

So, what is the swift code of Family Bank? The bank's BIC code is FABLKENAXXX. It can be broken down as follows:

  • FABL - Bank code
  • KE - Country code
  • NA - Location code
  • XXX - Branch code

What is the Family Bank routing number in Kenya?

A routing number is a nine-digit number that identifies the financial institution in a transaction and the location where your bank account was opened. For example, you'll often be asked for the checking account routing number when you're making a payment online or by phone.

The routing number for Family Bank is 261270679. You can find this number on the bottom left-hand side of any check issued by the bank.
