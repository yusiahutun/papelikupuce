How long was Johnny Joey Jones in the military?

Triple J was in the military for eight years. After completing his high school studies, he got a job as a forklift driver. After being disillusioned by life, he decided to enrol in the US Marine.

Shortly after being enlisted in the US Marine, he travelled to Parris Island in South Carolina, where he underwent basic training. He graduated and became a member of the United States Marine Corps.

He worked as an Explosive Ordnance Disposal Technician (bomb technician). His work was to assist in the disarmament and destruction of improvised explosive devices (IEDs). He served in the Iraq and Afghanistan wars.

What happened to Johnny Joey Jones?

During his time at the US Marines, Triple J was deployed on two combat tours in Iraq and Afghanistan. During his second combat tour in Afghanistan, he helped the military disarm over 80 IEDs.

He accidentally stepped on an IED during one of the disarming operations. The device exploded, resulting in serious injuries.

He lost both of his legs above the knee and suffered severe damage to his hands. The relevant authorities acted fast.

He was evacuated and treated at an army hospital in Landstuhl, Germany. He was later transferred to Walter Reed National Military Medical Centre for recovery.

The injuries forced him to go into early retirement from the service. It took him about ten months to physically recover. He learned to walk again.

Motivational speaking and non-profit work

Triple J sustained severe and life-changing injuries when he accidentally stepped on an IED. He started a peer visitation programme for military veterans while he was recovering at Walter Reed National Military Medical Centre.
