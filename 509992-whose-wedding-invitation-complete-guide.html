Whose name goes first on a wedding invitation? Complete guide

Nowadays, couples use creativity when wording their wedding invitation. Regardless of your creativity, the wording on a wedding invitation must always follow a specific format. For example, a wedding invitation must have the first and middle names of the bride and groom. Also, the bride and groom's first, middle, and last names must be present if the parents are not listed. But have you ever wondered whose name goes first on a wedding invitation?

A wedding invitation has a host line showing who is hosting the wedding. Traditionally, the wedding hosts were the parents whose names appeared on the host line.

In modern days, the host line includes the names of the bride and groom or a combination of the bride’s parents and groom’s parents. So, if you follow the modern format with only the bride and groom's names, does the man or woman's name go first on wedding invitations?

Whose name goes first on a wedding invitation?

Traditionally, the bride’s name came first on a wedding invitation. That tradition has continued for a long time. However, there are instances where the groom’s name is first.

In ancient times, the bride’s parents usually issued invitations. Thus, they used the first and middle names of the bride on the wedding invitations. However, the groom’s name was usually listed in full. Also, the groom’s title was included in the wording.

Today, there are situations where the bride and groom are the wedding hosts and require no input from parents. In this case, the couple can decide which name should come first on the wedding invitation. Also, they decide whether to use titles as part of the wording.

Why does the bride's name go first?

If you follow the traditional format, the bride’s name goes first on the wedding invitation because her parents are usually the hosts. Thus, they take the prestige of having their daughter’s name first because they pay a significant share of the expenses.

Where the bride’s parents are the hosts, they can also include the names of the groom’s parents. However, the names of the groom’s parents come second. In this instance, the wording on a wedding invitation may be as follows:

Alice & Michael Moore and Peter & Jane Lamb invite you to celebrate with their children, Grace & John, on their wedding day.

In the above example, Alice and Michael Moore are the bride’s parents; Peter and Jane Lamb are the groom’s parents, and Grace and John are the bride and groom.

In other wedding invitation examples where the bride and the groom are the hosts, the names of their parents are usually excluded. For example:

With great honour, we, Grace and John, invite you to celebrate our marriage.

The above two wedding invitation wording examples are different and go against tradition. Thus, many people prefer sticking to the tradition of making the bride’s name first on the wedding invitation.

How names can be addressed on wedding invitations

You can address names on wedding invitations in unique ways. Traditionally, many wedding invitation cards used the titles of each individual. The common titles are Mr, Mrs, Ms, Dr, Hon, and Eng.

The easiest way is to use Mr when inviting a male guest, followed by their full name. For female guests, use Ms followed by their full name. An exception comes when inviting a married couple.

In that case, you can address the duo as Mr and Mrs followed by their full names. If you plan to invite a politician, it is best to address them by their titles. For example, you can use Senator, Hon, Gov, or Your Excellency.

The tricky part comes when inviting widowed or divorced women. In this case, asking them how they should be addressed would be best. For a widowed woman, address them by using the honorific Mrs and her married name if she changed her last name to her husband’s or her maiden name if she never changed it.

For a divorced woman, you must determine whether she kept her married name. If she still has her married name, use Mrs to address her. But if she uses her maiden name, the best way is to use Ms along with her first and maiden name.

How many lines are in a wedding invitation?

A wedding invitation can have as many or as fewer lines as possible. But when following the traditional format, the card has six lines, including:

  • Host line – The people hosting the wedding, typically the bride’s parents.
  • Request line – Asking the invited people to attend the wedding.
  • Bride and groom names – The bride's first name followed by the groom's full name.
  • Date and time of the event – The date and time of the wedding.
  • The location of the event – The location of the wedding.
  • The reception location – The reception location is optional but should be included as the last line.

Understand that the lines may be fewer, especially if the hosts are the bride and groom and you include the event's data, time, and location on the same line. But sticking to the traditional format is much better.
